Charlotte’s Web [11]

Jul 25, 2023

Answer

Lurvy felt weak. He brushed his hand across his eyes and stared harder at Charlotte's web.

"I'm seeing things," he whispered. He dropped to his knees and uttered a short prayer.

Zuckerman stared at the writing on the web. Then he murmured the words "Some Pig." Then he looked at Lurvy. Then they both began to tremble.

"Mrs. Zuckerman sank into a chair. She looked pale and frightened.

When a small fly blundered into the web, just beyond the word “pig,” Charlotte dropped quickly down, rolled the fly up, and carried it out of the way.

She sat without moving a muscle, and listened to the conversation of the people.

"Some pig!" muttered Lurvy in a low voice.

"Some pig!" Whispered Mr. Zuckerman.

"Edith, something has happened," he said, in a weak voice.

"Edith," he said, trying to keep his voice steady.

The grass looked like a magic carpet. The asparagus patch looked like a silver forest.

The web glistened3 in the light and made a pattern of loveliness and mystery, like a delicate veil.

There, in the center of the web, neatly5 woven in block letters, was a message.

A look of complete bewilderment came over Mrs. Zuckerman's face.
